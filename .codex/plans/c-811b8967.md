## Risks

- Persisted status transitions provide exact queue membership but not a formally guaranteed asyncio semaphore position; the output should not promise numbered execution order without a larger scheduler redesign.
- `queued_checks` is counted by the existing admission limit even though check polling normally runs outside the Codex semaphore; including it preserves current queue-capacity semantics, while excluding `waiting_checks` avoids presenting passive CI waits as Codex work.
- Status sets currently exist implicitly across service transitions and SQL. If they are duplicated instead of centralized, future phases could be admitted but omitted from the queue display, or vice versa.
- A service-wide listing could expose repository and issue activity across otherwise isolated Telegram chats/topics; a scoped listing can hide jobs elsewhere that consume the global queue capacity.
- `/ask` uses a separate, non-persisted task registry and semaphore. Including it would require a broader queue abstraction and restart semantics rather than simply querying `code_jobs`.
